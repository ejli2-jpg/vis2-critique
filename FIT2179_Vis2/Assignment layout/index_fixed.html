FIT2179 · Australian Tourism · 2024

Australia's visitor economy is one of the country's most dynamic industries

Shaped by millions of journeys taken every year — from weekend getaways in regional Victoria to long-haul flights landing in Sydney from Tokyo, London, and New Delhi — this visualisation explores where Australians travel within their own country, which nations send the most visitors to our shores, what those visitors do and spend when they arrive, and how the industry has recovered from the most devastating disruption in its history — the COVID-19 pandemic. The picture that emerges is one of remarkable scale, stark geographic inequality, and an uneven recovery that has left some markets thriving and others still finding their footing.

The big picture — scale, trend, and the COVID collapse

Before diving into detail, it helps to understand the scale. In 2024, Australia recorded 7.6 million international visitor trips and 113 million domestic overnight trips. The charts below show how those numbers have moved over time and how the pandemic created the sharpest single-year collapse in Australian tourism history.

Section 1 — the big picture

Scale, trend, and the COVID collapse

Domestic tourism dwarfs international in raw trip numbers, but international visitors punch well above their weight on spend per night. The dot map shows the visitor night split between domestic and international across every state, while the charts below reveal how the pandemic hollowed out arrivals before a slow and uneven recovery began in 2022.

Why they travel and what they do when they get here

Purpose shapes spending patterns dramatically. International education tourism drives enormous spend per trip, while domestic holiday travel dominates in raw volume. Australia's natural environment — beaches, national parks, wildlife — remains the defining experiential drawcard.

When they visit — and are we back to normal?

International arrivals collapsed from 9.4 million in 2019 to just 263,000 in 2021 — a 97% decline. The recovery has been uneven. New Zealand and the USA have returned to or exceeded pre-pandemic levels. China remains well below its 2019 peak. The state recovery map closes the story geographically.
